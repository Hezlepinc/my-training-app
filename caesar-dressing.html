Caesar Dressing

Real Caesar — egg yolk, anchovy, garlic, Parmesan, lemon. Immersion blender. Better than restaurants.

Ingredients

  • 1 large
    Egg yolk, room temperaturewhole egg works for a lighter version
  • 1 cup
    Avocado oil
  • 1 tbsp
    Anchovy pasteor 4-5 oil-packed filets, finely mashed
  • 4 cloves
    Garlic, grated on a microplane
  • 2 tbsp
    Fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp
    Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp
    Worcestershire sauce (optional)
  • 1/3 cup
    Grated Parmesanfreshly grated; Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • 1/2 tsp
    Black pepper, freshly ground
  • to taste
    Saltanchovies and Parmesan are salty — taste before adding any

Instructions

  1. In a tall narrow jar (wide-mouth pint mason ideal), combine egg yolk, anchovy paste, grated garlic, lemon juice, Dijon, Worcestershire, black pepper. Pour avocado oil on top.
  2. Insert immersion blender to the bottom of the jar. Don't lift yet. Turn on full blast for 20 seconds — emulsion forms from the bottom up.
  3. Slowly raise the blender (5 seconds) while still running to incorporate the rest of the oil.
  4. Stop blending. Fold in grated Parmesan by hand with a spoon — don't blend it in or it gets gummy.
  5. Taste. Adjust: more lemon for brightness, more anchovy for depth, salt if needed, more pepper for bite.
  6. Jar it. Refrigerate. Lasts 1 week (raw egg).
DIY Batch Prep Plan
Sunday batch: standard recipe → 1-2 mason jars. Use on chopped romaine + Parmesan curls + grilled chicken for a classic Caesar salad lunch.
Bonus uses: drizzle over roasted broccoli, dip for grilled wings, marinade for chicken thighs before searing.
Why this beats store-bought Caesar
Bottled Caesar dressings universally use soybean oil + sugar + preservatives. Even "premium" brands sneak in canola. Real Caesar is one of the simplest gourmet dressings to make — anchovy is the secret weapon and pure avocado oil is the base. Don't skip the anchovy thinking it'll be fishy — it dissolves into umami and tastes nothing like fish.
