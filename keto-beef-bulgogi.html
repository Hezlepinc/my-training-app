Keto Beef Bulgogi

Korean BBQ ribeye with garlic-ginger-sesame marinade. 30 minutes + marinade. Cleaned up — monk fruit replaces brown sugar.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb
    Ribeye, thinly slicedfreeze 30 min before slicing for 1/8" thickness; or ask butcher for "shabu shabu cut"
  • 1/4 cup
    Coconut aminosreplaces soy sauce — milder, slightly sweeter
  • 2 tbsp
    Sesame oil (toasted, dark)
  • 2 tbsp
    Monk fruit / erythritol blendLakanto Golden mimics brown sugar; NEVER use sucralose
  • 6 cloves
    Garlic, finely minced or grated
  • 1 tbsp
    Fresh ginger, grated
  • 2
    Green onions, sliced (whites + greens separated)
  • 1 tsp
    Red pepper flakes (gochugaru if available)
  • 1 tbsp
    Rice vinegar (unseasoned)
  • 2 tbsp
    Toasted sesame seeds (garnish)
  • 2 tbsp
    Avocado oil (for searing)

Instructions

  1. Whisk marinade: coconut aminos, sesame oil, monk fruit, garlic, ginger, white parts of green onions, red pepper flakes, rice vinegar.
  2. Add sliced ribeye, toss to fully coat. Marinate at least 30 minutes (2-4 hours ideal). Beef gets more tender the longer it marinates.
  3. Heat a large cast iron skillet or wok over high heat until smoking. Add avocado oil.
  4. Add beef in a single layer in batches (don't crowd the pan — that steams it instead of searing). Cook 60-90 seconds per side until edges char and beef is just cooked through.
  5. Remove each batch to a plate. After all batches, pour any remaining marinade into the hot pan, let it bubble and reduce 60 seconds, then pour over the cooked beef.
  6. Garnish with green parts of green onions and toasted sesame seeds. Serve immediately.
Meal Plan Fit
Meal 1 (noon, post-WOD): 1 serving over cauliflower rice + kimchi + 1/2 avocado = ~700 cal / 8g net carbs. Excellent ribeye-forward refuel after a hard fasted training session.
Lettuce wrap option: Bibb lettuce cups + bulgogi + sliced cucumber + sesame seeds. Lower-cal, higher-volume for a satisfying meal without piling on calories.
Clean-keto swaps applied
Brown sugar → monk fruit / erythritol blend. Traditional bulgogi uses 2-3 tbsp brown sugar per pound (~25g carbs added). Lakanto Golden monk fruit mimics the molasses note of brown sugar without any blood sugar impact. Also swapped: soy sauce → coconut aminos, sesame oil for cooking high-heat → avocado oil for the sear (sesame oil only as a finishing/flavor oil), "seasoned" rice vinegar → unseasoned (no added sugar). NEVER use bottled bulgogi marinade — they all contain corn syrup or sugar.
