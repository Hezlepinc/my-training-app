Electrolytes — Non-Negotiable (tap to check off, resets each new dose)

Sodium 3,000–5,000 mg/day
Salt water (¼ tsp pink salt in 12 oz water). Salt MORE than feels normal.
Potassium 1,000–3,500 mg/day
LMNT or potassium chloride (Lite Salt) — zero-sugar, zero-calorie.
Magnesium 300–400 mg (evening)
Magnesium glycinate before bed. Prevents cramps.
Water — 3+ liters today
Pure fast = zero calories. Only water, black coffee, plain tea & electrolytes. Drink on a schedule, not just when thirsty.

🔴 Stop the Fast NOW If…

  • Dizziness that doesn't resolve after salt water + sitting
  • Extreme weakness / graying out when standing
  • Sharp headache electrolytes don't fix in 20 min
  • Heart palpitations or irregular beat
  • Confusion or disorientation
Normal — push through: hunger waves (pass in minutes), mild headache (electrolytes fix it), boredom, mildly cold hands. No ego — break early if a red signal hits. Breaking the fast is never failure.

⚕️ Medical Caution

Skip extended fasting (or only do it under medical supervision) if you are: diabetic on medication · have a history of eating disorders · have gout · pregnant · underweight.

Hydration: thirst can be blunted on a long fast — drink on a schedule, not just when thirsty. Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) are non-negotiable (see above).

Breaking the Fast (matters as much as the fast)

1
Ease in slow — a few sips of salt water, then ½ avocado or a small handful of macadamia nuts.
WAIT 20–30 MIN
2
Something small & gentle — 2–3 boiled eggs, a few oz salmon, or ½ avocado.
WAIT 1 HOUR
3
Then a normal keto meal. Never break a long fast with a big fatty meal — cramps and nausea.

No Training

Walking only — actually helps. No WOD, no lifting, no metcon during an extended fast in adaptation phase. Ease back in the day after you break.
