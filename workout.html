Training

6-day protocol · fixed structure, rotating content · keto fat-loss phase

The whole idea: the 6 day-slots never change — that's your consistency. What rotates is the content inside each slot (which WOD, which run, which accessories). You never redesign the week; you pull the next variation from the movement bank. This is what makes it last for years. Tap a day to expand it. Tap the circle to mark it done this week.

The Week

Monday
Bodyweight
Engine + relative strength + joints
▶
Warm-up (8 min)
Jump rope 3 min · then 2 rounds: 10 air squats, 10 push-ups, 10 walking lunges, 5 inchworms, arm circles + leg swings.
Main (pick 1 from Mon bank)
Bodyweight conditioning, 20–35 min. Cindy-style AMRAPs, EMOMs, or chippers. Pull from the Monday Bodyweight bank below — rotate weekly.
Target Zone 3–4 (steady, working but sustainable)
Finisher (optional, 5 min)
Plank ladder or hollow holds + superman. Core.
Scaling: sub jumping pull-ups or ring rows for strict; knee push-ups late in rounds; step-ups for box jumps. Walk to recover — that's pacing, not failure.
Tuesday
Lower — Squat Strength
Strength + legs/glutes · HIGH
▶
Warm-up
5 min easy treadmill/row · empty bar 2×10, build in sets to working weight.
Main lift — Back Squat (wave progression)
  • Weeks 1–3: 5×5 @ ~70–75% 1RM (RPE 7) — add a little each week while bar speed stays crisp
  • Heavy week: 4×4 @ ~80–85% (RPE 8)
  • Back-off / deload: 3×5 @ ~65%, then restart the wave one notch higher
Pick a working weight where the last rep is solid — never a grind. Strength stimulus without redlining the engine.
Accessories (hypertrophy — 8–15 reps)
  • Bulgarian split squats — 3×10/leg (DBs)
  • Romanian deadlifts — 3×10 (barbell, glutes/hams)
  • Walking lunges — 3×12/leg (DBs)
  • Calf raises — 3×15–20 (off plyo box edge)
8–15 rep range = the muscle-preserving / shaping zone in a deficit.
Fasted AM: if bar speed tanks during early adaptation, 5g BCAA pre-session helps. Drop to 4×3 if recovery is poor that week.
Wednesday
Medium Run — 2–3 mi
Aerobic base · fat-loss pace
▶
The run
2–3 miles at a conversational pace — you should be able to speak in short sentences. This is an engine-builder, not a race.
Zone 3 · fat-burning
Why slow
When cardio is the limiter, long Zone 3 grinders build the aerobic base that makes everything else easier — WODs, mud runs, recovery. Walk the hills if heart rate spikes. That's correct pacing.
Progression
Hold 2–3 mi at steady pace for 3–4 weeks, then either add ½ mile OR add a 4×1 min tempo pickup in the middle. Same slot, never harder than your engine can hold.
Outdoor preferred (terrain = mud-run carryover). Treadmill in bad weather.
Thursday
Upper Body
Push / pull hypertrophy
▶
Warm-up
Band pull-aparts + shoulder dislocates · push-ups 2×10 · light bench sets building up.
Main lift — Bench Press (wave, mirrors Tue)
  • Weeks 1–3: 5×5 @ ~70–75% 1RM, building
  • Heavy week: 4×4 @ ~80–85%
  • Back-off: 3×5 @ ~65%, restart one notch up
Pull + accessories (8–15 reps)
  • Pull-ups — 4×max (or negatives / banded) — the back-width mover
  • One-arm DB row — 3×10/arm
  • DB shoulder press — 3×10
  • DB curls — 3×12 · Overhead triceps ext — 3×12
  • Lateral raises — 3×15 — shoulder caps drive the "fit" look
Push/pull balance keeps shoulders healthy long-term. Pull-up volume drives the V-taper that reads lean even mid-cut.
Friday
WOD
Mixed-modal · CrossFit baseline · HIGH
▶
Format
Run ladder + couplet/triplet between (e.g. 200/400/800/400/200 m with rounds of work between), or a classic benchmark. ~30–40 min of work. Pull from the Friday WOD bank below.
Zone 4–5 OK here · this is the hard day
Barbell weight
Keep it light. On a WOD the engine should be the limiter, not the bar — this protects pacing and joints.
This is the day that keeps you "mud-run / drop-in ready." If you only protected two hard sessions a week, this + Tuesday squat are them. Scale reps, not intensity.
Saturday
Long Run
Engine · fat-loss grinder
▶
The run
Start at 3–4 miles, build toward 5–6 over months. Slow and steady — the single biggest engine-builder in the week.
Zone 2–3 · easy aerobic
Walk-run is the strategy, not a fallback
Run until heart rate climbs, walk until it settles, repeat. Over weeks the run intervals lengthen on their own. That's exactly how the engine grows.
Progression
Add ~½ mile every 2–3 weeks until ~6 mi, then hold and add pace. A 6-mile easy base = you could jog a mud run tomorrow without training for it.
Fasted long runs are deep into fat-burning — keep electrolytes up (salt + water before, broth after).
Sunday
Rest / Mobility
Recovery — makes 6 days survivable
▶
Do
Easy 20–30 min walk (Zone 1–2) · 10 min mobility on whatever's tight (hips, shoulders, ankles) · foam roll.
Don't
No lifting, no metcon. The whole 6-day plan works because Sunday is real recovery. Skip it and the wheels come off by week 6.

Progression & Deload

Strength (Tue/Thu): main lift waves up over 3–4 week blocks (5×5 → 4×4 heavier → back-off), then repeats one notch higher. Add weight OR reps — never both at once. Structure never changes.

Runs (Wed/Sat): extend distance or add tempo pickups month over month. Same slots forever.

WOD / Bodyweight (Fri/Mon): pull the next variation from the banks above. Fresh every week, format fixed.

Deload every 4–6 weeks: one lighter week — drop volume ~40% (cut sets, lighten bar, shorten runs). Joint insurance. You come back stronger, not behind.

Fasted-AM + Keto Notes

All sessions fasted AM (before the eating window).

The two hard fasted days (Tue squat, Fri WOD): optional 5g BCAA pre-session during early adaptation (first 2–3 weeks); scale intensity back ~20% while adapting.

Electrolytes before every session — pinch of salt + water, magnesium at night. Same targets as the fast tracker.

Rower note: if shoes slip the stirrups, use flat-soled shoes or just socks — or sub bike / run / jump rope. Don't let it block a WOD.
