Indian Butter Chicken

Murgh Makhani, naturally keto-shaped. Tomato-cream sauce, butter, ghee, warm spices.

Ingredients

  • 2 lb
    Boneless skinless chicken thighscut into 1.5-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup
    Full-fat plain Greek yogurtfor marinade — Fage 5% or similar
  • 2 tbsp
    Garam masala
  • 1 tbsp
    Ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp
    Paprika
  • 1 tsp
    Turmeric
  • 1 tbsp
    Fresh grated ginger
  • 6 cloves
    Garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp
    Gheeor grass-fed butter
  • 4 tbsp
    Grass-fed butter
  • 1/2 cup
    Tomato pasteno added sugar — most are fine; check label
  • 1 cup
    Heavy whipping cream
  • 1 tsp
    Salt
  • 1/4 cup
    Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Marinade: combine yogurt, half the garam masala, half the cumin, half the paprika, turmeric, ginger, half the garlic, and 1 tsp salt. Add chicken, toss to coat. Marinate 30 min (or up to 24 hours in fridge).
  2. Heat ghee in a large skillet over medium-high. Add marinated chicken in batches, sear 3-4 min per side until charred edges form. Remove to plate (cooking will finish in sauce).
  3. Reduce heat to medium. Add butter to skillet. When melted, add remaining garlic, garam masala, cumin, paprika. Toast 60 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add tomato paste. Cook 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly — this caramelizes the paste and is critical for flavor depth.
  5. Pour in heavy cream, whisking until smooth. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  6. Return chicken (and any accumulated juices) to skillet. Reduce heat to low, simmer uncovered 8-10 minutes until chicken is cooked through and sauce thickens.
  7. Taste and adjust salt. Garnish with cilantro. Serve over cauliflower rice.
Meal Plan Fit
Meal 1 (noon, post-WOD): 1 serving over cauliflower rice with sautéed spinach = ~870 cal / 10g net carbs. Outstanding post-fasted-WOD refuel — high protein + high fat in one bowl.
Meal prep: Doubles or triples cleanly. Sauce gets better on day 2. Refrigerate 4 days, freeze 3 months.
Why this is clean
Ghee + butter + heavy cream + yogurt for fat. Whole spices (no curry powder blends that may contain anti-caking fillers). Real tomato paste (one ingredient: tomatoes). No store-bought "butter chicken sauce" jars — those often contain sugar, sunflower oil, modified food starch.
