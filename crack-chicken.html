Crack Chicken (Slow Cooker)

Chicken + cream cheese + cheddar + bacon + homemade ranch. The "doesn't taste keto" benchmark. Cleaned up from standard recipe.

Instructions

  1. Place chicken breasts in the slow cooker. Sprinkle ranch seasoning over the top, then pour in broth.
  2. Distribute cream cheese chunks over the chicken. Cover.
  3. Cook on LOW for 4-6 hours, or HIGH for 2-3 hours, until chicken shreds easily with a fork.
  4. Remove chicken to a plate. Whisk the liquid in the slow cooker to fully incorporate the now-melted cream cheese into a sauce.
  5. Shred chicken with two forks. Return to slow cooker and toss in the sauce until every piece is coated.
  6. Stir in 3/4 of the bacon and 3/4 of the cheddar. Let melt 5 minutes with the lid off.
  7. Top with remaining bacon, cheddar, and green onions. Serve over cauliflower rice, in chaffle sandwiches, in lettuce wraps, or piled on a chaffle as a melt.
Meal Plan Fit
Meal 1 (noon, post-WOD): 1 serving over cauliflower rice with sautéed spinach = ~520 cal / 5g net carbs. High protein refuel.
Meal prep king: Makes 6 servings. Refrigerates 4 days, freezes 2 months. Reheats with a splash of broth to bring sauce back. Perfect for the W2 → post-W2 transition when meal prep matters most.
Clean-keto swaps applied
Hidden Valley Ranch packet → homemade ranch seasoning. Standard recipe calls for the HV packet which contains maltodextrin, modified food starch, sugar, dextrose, and (in most versions) MSG. The homemade blend takes 2 minutes to mix once and lasts months. Also swapped: standard bacon → sugar-free uncured, pre-shredded cheese → block cheese shredded fresh.
