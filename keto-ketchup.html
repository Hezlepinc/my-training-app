DIY Keto Ketchup

Whisk → simmer → cool. Allspice + cloves + cinnamon are what makes it taste like ketchup, not pasta sauce.

Ingredients

  • 6 oz
    Tomato paste, no added sugar1 small can — check label, NOT "tomato sauce"
  • 1/2 cup
    Water
  • 3 tbsp
    Apple cider vinegarBragg's raw/unfiltered
  • 1-2 tbsp
    Powdered erythritol or monk fruitLakanto; start with 1 tbsp, taste, add more if needed
  • 1 tsp
    Salt
  • 1/2 tsp
    Onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp
    Garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp
    Ground allspicethe signature ketchup spice
  • pinch
    Ground clovesdon't skip — it's what makes ketchup taste like ketchup
  • pinch
    Ground cinnamon (optional)adds warmth; can skip

Instructions

  1. Whisk all ingredients in a small saucepan over medium-low heat until smooth.
  2. Bring to a very gentle simmer (small bubbles around the edges, not boiling). Whisk occasionally for 5-10 minutes until it thickens to ketchup consistency. It will thicken more as it cools.
  3. Taste and adjust: more sweetener for sweeter, more salt for depth, more ACV for tang, more allspice for more "ketchup" character.
  4. Cool fully (15 minutes on counter, or quick-cool over an ice bath) before jarring.
  5. Transfer to a clean mason jar with a lid. Refrigerate.
  6. Keeps 3 weeks in the fridge.
DIY Batch Prep Plan
Sunday batch: double the recipe (2 cans tomato paste, 1 cup water, etc.) → 2 mason jars, ~3 weeks of ketchup.
Replaces: Primal Kitchen Unsweetened Ketchup. Used in bacon cheeseburger casserole. Goes great with eggs, burgers, meatloaf.
Why this beats store-bought "keto" ketchup
G Hughes, Heinz "No Sugar Added," and similar brands use sucralose + acesulfame K — both flagged for gut microbiome impact. Primal Kitchen Unsweetened is clean but costs $7 and is hard to find. This DIY costs ~$2 and you control the sweetness. The trick is the spice blend (allspice + cloves) — that's the secret ingredient that elevates it from "tomato condiment" to actual ketchup.
