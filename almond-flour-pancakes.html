Almond Flour Pancakes

Light, fluffy, butter-cooked. 7.5g net carbs per serving including berries.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup
    Almond flourblanched, fine grind — NOT almond meal
  • 4 large
    Eggspasture-raised, room temp
  • 2 tbsp
    Heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tbsp
    Grass-fed butter, melted (for batter)
  • 1 tsp
    Baking powder
  • 1 tsp
    Vanilla extractpure, not imitation
  • 1-2 tbsp
    Monk fruit / erythritol blendLakanto or Swerve — NEVER use sucralose-blend "Splenda Naturals"
  • Pinch
    Sea salt
  • 2 tbsp
    Grass-fed butter (for cooking)
  • 1/4 cup
    Fresh raspberries or blackberries (topping)small handful — 5-8g net per cup

Instructions

  1. Whisk eggs in a medium bowl until smooth. Add cream, melted butter, vanilla. Whisk to combine.
  2. Add almond flour, baking powder, sweetener, salt. Stir until just combined — don't over-mix. Let batter rest 3 minutes (this is critical for fluffy texture — almond flour needs to hydrate).
  3. Heat a non-stick or cast iron skillet over medium-LOW heat. Almond flour browns fast — keep heat below where wheat pancakes would cook.
  4. Add 1/2 tbsp butter to skillet. When melted, scoop 1/4 cup batter per pancake. Cook 2-3 minutes until small bubbles form and edges look set.
  5. Flip gently with a thin spatula. Cook 90 seconds more. Almond flour pancakes are more delicate than wheat — flip carefully or two-stage flip with two spatulas.
  6. Transfer to plate, repeat with remaining batter, adding butter between batches.
  7. Top each serving with a tablespoon of butter and a small handful of berries. Skip the syrup unless you have a sugar-free option (most "sugar-free" pancake syrups use sucralose — not approved).
DeLauer Plan Fit
Meal 1 (noon, post-WOD) — Saturday breakfast option: 2 pancakes + 4oz bacon + 2 eggs = ~1100 cal / 9g net carbs. Hits fat target hard, satisfies carb-replacement craving without spiking insulin.
Don't make these daily. Almond flour is high in omega-6; treat as a 1-2x/week option, not a default breakfast.
Why this is clean
Only erythritol or monk fruit sweetener (NEVER sucralose, NEVER maltitol). Real butter for cooking — not vegetable oil "buttery spread." Pure vanilla extract, not imitation (which can contain corn syrup). Skip flavored "keto pancake mixes" — most contain modified food starch and undisclosed sweeteners.
